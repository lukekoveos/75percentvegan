## Executive Summary

The "Health" section is the missing piece of the 75percentvegan.org puzzle. Right now the site nails the *why* (Philosophy), the *how* (Recipes), and the *culture* (Music, Blog). What it doesn't have is a science-backed answer to the most common question curious readers actually Google: **"Is eating less meat actually good for me?"**

This section doesn't moralize. It doesn't say "meat will kill you." It says: here's what the peer-reviewed science shows happens when you eat more plants. You decide what to do with that.

Tone: Direct, slightly smug about the science but never preachy. Grounded in real studies, not feel-good fluff.

## Research Summary: The Good Stuff

### Meat Reduction & Chronic Disease (The Headline Numbers)

**Lancet Planetary Health, July 2024** (microsimulation study, US adult population):
- A **30% reduction in processed meat** alone = estimated **352,900 fewer type 2 diabetes cases** over 10 years
- Same reduction = **92,500 fewer cardiovascular disease cases**
- Same reduction = **53,300 fewer colorectal cancer cases**
- A **30% reduction in unprocessed red meat** = additional **382,400 fewer CVD cases** (per Harvard Health summary, Oct 2024)
- Source: *The Lancet Planetary Health*, PIIS2542-5196(24)00118-9

**Key insight for 75% framing:** The Lancet study modelled *30% reduction* — which is basically the 75% approach in numerical terms. This is not coincidence. This is the thesis.

**BMC Medicine / Springer Nature (2023):**
- Systematic review & meta-analysis of prospective studies
- Substituting animal-based with plant-based foods = lower risk of CVD, type 2 diabetes, all-cause mortality
- Source: BMC Medicine, doi:10.1186/s12916-023-03093-1

**PLOS One (2024):**
- Vegetarian/vegan diets significantly improve metabolic profile: reduced total and LDL cholesterol, fasting blood glucose, HbA1c
- Source: PLOS One, doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0300711

### Fiber & Gut Health (The Gut-Brain Angle)

**The 30-Plant Rule** (Tim Spector / ZOE research):
- People who eat **30+ different plants per week** have significantly more "good" gut bacteria than those eating 10
- Plants contain polyphenols and prebiotics which feed the gut microbiome
- Source: ZOE/Tim Spector research; Guardian interview May 2022; Business Insider May 2024

**Cambridge Core / PMC — Dietary Fibre & Gut-Brain Axis (2021/2024):**
- Cross-sectional and prospective cohort studies show **positive associations between higher dietary fibre intake and better cognitive and affective processes**
- Higher fibre = better mental health outcomes (mood, anxiety reduction)
- Source: *Gut Microbiome* (Cambridge), doi:10.1017/gmi.2021.14; PMC11406392

**The Fiber Gap (Alarming Stat):**
- **95% of Americans are deficient in fiber** — primarily because of low fruit, veg, legume and whole grain intake
- Animal products contain zero fiber
- Source: PMC8210981 ("A Look at Plant-Based Diets")

**Cognitive Impairment / Fiber Deprivation:**
- A fiber-deprived diet causes cognitive impairment via hippocampal microglia-mediated synaptic loss
- Source: *Microbiome* (Springer), doi:10.1186/s40168-021-01172-0

**Longevity:**
- Fiber intake, regular exercise, and pre/probiotic supplements are key interventions to foster vitality in later years via gut microbiome
- Source: *Journal of Biomedical Science*, doi:10.1186/s12929-025-01179-x

### Cooking as Therapy

**PMC Systematic Review (Farmer, Touchton-Leonard & Ross, 2018):**
- Cooking interventions showed improvements in: self-esteem, social interaction, anxiety reduction, psychological well-being, quality of life
- Source: PMC5862744

**PERMA Model & Cooking (PMC 2021):**
- Cooking is an evolutionarily essential behavior for psychosocial health
- Cooking connects Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, and Accomplishment
- Source: PMC8071848

**Occupational Therapy Research:**
- Cooking requires simultaneous physical and mental skills — shown to decrease risk of disability and cognitive decline in older adults
- Source: Dr. Axe / rehabilitation therapy literature (draxe.com, 2022)

**Australian 7-Week Cooking Program (2022):**
- Improved cooking confidence, ability to change eating habits, behavioral change around food, overcoming lifestyle barriers
- Source: ReachLink/Australian research team, 2022

### Music & Health

**PMC / Frontiers in Psychology — Music and Stress:**
- Music listening **reduces cortisol levels** and sympathetic nervous system activity
- Works by influencing both the hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal (HPA) axis and the autonomous nervous system
- After a stressor, music listening reduced cortisol levels **more rapidly** than silence
- Source: PMC3734071; Frontiers in Psychology, doi:10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1108402; Khalfa et al. 2003

**Music and Immune Function:**
- Positive emotional states stimulated by music are linked to improved immune system markers
- Source: HeartMath Institute research library; PMC3110826 (music during surgery → IgA levels)

**PLOS One (Scheider et al. 2013):**
- Music measurably suppresses stress-related cortisol increase
- Source: PLOS One, doi:10.1371/journal.pone.0070156

## Section Structure Recommendation

### Decision: Top-Level Navigation Section ✅

**Recommendation: "Health" should be a new top-level content type in the nav**, not a category within Blog.

**Reasoning:**
1. **Volume:** 8 articles warrant their own section
2. **Distinct audience intent:** People searching health topics aren't in "blog" mode — they want authoritative, findable content
3. **SEO cluster:** A `/health/` URL base (with pillar page at the root) is cleaner than `/blog/health/` for building topical authority
4. **Brand positioning:** Having Health as a top-level section alongside Recipes signals that this is a serious, research-backed site — not just a recipe blog with opinions
5. **Navigation balance:** Currently: Recipes, Blog, Philosophy, Music. Adding Health gives the nav a more complete picture of the movement

**Hugo implementation:**
- Create `/content/health/` content directory
- Create `/content/health/_index.md` as the section page
- Articles live at `/content/health/article-slug.md`
- Update `config.toml` / `hugo.toml` navigation menu to include Health
- Add `categories: ["health"]` to frontmatter for taxonomy support

**Nav order suggestion:** Recipes | Health | Blog | Philosophy | Music
*(Health after Recipes makes sense — people who find a recipe may want to understand the why)*

## Tone Guide for Writers

**The voice is:** Confident science communicator who finds this stuff genuinely interesting. Not a dietitian writing a pamphlet. Not a vegan activist.

**Do:**
- Lead with surprising or counterintuitive facts
- Name the studies (Lancet, PMC, Cambridge Core) — authority matters
- Frame benefits as things you gain ("your gut microbiome gets more diverse") not things you avoid
- Acknowledge nuance (e.g., "the evidence on unprocessed red meat is less clear-cut")
- Make it practical — every article should end with "here's what this means you could actually do"
- Use dry humour sparingly but effectively

**Don't:**
- Say "meat is bad for you" — say "here's what the data shows about risk reduction"
- Be preachy about animal welfare (that's Philosophy's job)
- Overclaim — nutrition science has real uncertainty, acknowledge it
- Use jargon without explaining it
- Pad word count with filler

**Signature line examples:**
> "The science doesn't require your opinion. It's happening in your bloodstream regardless."

> "You don't have to believe in plant-based eating. Your gut bacteria are already voting."

> "Nobody's asking you to become a different person. Just eat more things with roots."
